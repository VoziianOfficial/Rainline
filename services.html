Comparison mindset

A gutter request should make provider follow-up easier.

The right provider conversation depends on the project type. New installation questions are different from cleaning, repair, or replacement questions. Rainline helps homeowners start with a clearer request.

01

Describe the current gutter condition

Mention whether the gutters are missing, clogged, leaking, rusted, detached, overflowing, or being planned for a new home exterior update.

02

Share project location and access details

Provider availability may vary by ZIP code, home height, roofline access, weather, and project complexity.

03

Compare written provider details

Before hiring, review quote scope, materials, cleanup, timing, warranties, license, insurance, and payment terms directly with the provider.

Before moving forward

What to ask independent gutter providers.

What materials, profiles, and downspout options are included?

Does the quote include removal, cleanup, hardware, and disposal?

Are license and insurance documents available for review?

What timeline, payment terms, and warranty information apply?

How will rainwater be directed away from the home?
