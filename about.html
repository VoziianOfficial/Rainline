Company story

Rainline was built for the moment before homeowners know who to call.

Gutter projects rarely begin with a perfect project brief. They often start with a small sign: water spilling over an edge, a loose section after a storm, leaves blocking flow, or an aging system that no longer feels reliable.

Rainline was created to make that first step more organized. Instead of acting like a local contractor, the platform helps homeowners describe the request, choose the right gutter service category, and compare independent local provider options with better context.

The goal is not to replace homeowner judgment. The goal is to make the beginning of the comparison process clearer, calmer, and easier to navigate.

Rainline is not a gutter contractor. Homeowners should verify license, insurance, written quote details, service scope, and provider terms directly before hiring.

Platform FAQ

Clear answers about Rainline’s role in the gutter provider search.

01

Is Rainline a gutter contractor?

No. Rainline does not install, replace, repair, clean, inspect, or maintain gutters directly.

02

What does Rainline actually do?

Rainline helps homeowners organize project details and start a request for independent local provider options.

03

Does Rainline choose the provider for me?

No. Homeowners should compare provider details directly and make their own hiring decision.

04

Are providers independent?

Yes. Providers are independent businesses. Rainline does not control their pricing, scheduling, materials, or work quality.

05

What should I verify before hiring?

Verify license, insurance, written quote scope, warranty details, payment terms, and service limitations.

06

Can availability vary by location?

Yes. Provider availability can depend on ZIP code, season, project type, home access, and local coverage.

Important clarity

Rainline is a matching platform, not the company doing the gutter work.

Any provider a homeowner may speak with through a matching flow is independent. Rainline does not control final pricing, appointment availability, project scope, materials, warranties, work quality, insurance status, or licensing. Those details should be confirmed directly before hiring.
